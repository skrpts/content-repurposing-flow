## LinkedIn

- **Character limits:** Posts up to 3,000 characters; articles up to 125,000 characters; headline 220 characters
- **Image dimensions:** Single image 1200 × 1200 px (square) or 1200 × 627 px (landscape); carousel slides 1080 × 1080 px (PDF upload); cover image 1584 × 396 px
- **Best posting times:** Tuesday to Thursday, 8–10 am and 12–1 pm (audience's local time); avoid weekends
- **Hashtag conventions:** 3–5 targeted hashtags; place at the end of the post or in the first comment; use a mix of broad (#Marketing) and niche (#B2BSaaSContent) tags
- **Content formats:** Text posts, single image, document carousels (PDF), native video, articles, polls, newsletters
- **Audience expectations:** Professional, insight-driven content. Personal stories perform well when tied to a professional lesson. Self-promotional posts without value get penalised by the algorithm. Long-form posts (800–1,500 characters) with line breaks tend to outperform short updates.
- **Engagement patterns:** Comments carry more weight than reactions. Posts that prompt genuine discussion (questions, contrarian takes, "agree or disagree" framing) see the best reach. The first hour after posting is critical — reply to every comment quickly.

## X / Twitter

- **Character limits:** 280 characters per post (free tier); 25,000 characters (Premium+); threads are unlimited in length
- **Image dimensions:** Single image 1600 × 900 px (16:9) or 1080 × 1080 px (square); up to 4 images per post
- **Best posting times:** Weekdays 8–10 am and 5–6 pm; Monday and Thursday tend to see highest engagement
- **Hashtag conventions:** 1–2 hashtags maximum; overuse looks spammy; place inline within the text rather than stacking at the end
- **Content formats:** Short text, threads, images, GIFs, polls, Spaces (live audio), video (up to 2 min 20 sec free, longer for Premium)
- **Audience expectations:** Concise, opinionated, conversational. Threads are the long-form format here — break complex ideas into 4–8 tweet chunks with a strong hook in the first post. Humour, hot takes, and timely commentary perform well. Overly polished corporate language underperforms.
- **Engagement patterns:** Retweets/reposts drive reach more than likes. Quote tweets with added commentary spark conversation. Replying to your own thread with additional context boosts visibility. The algorithm favours posts that generate replies within the first 30 minutes.

## Instagram

- **Character limits:** Captions up to 2,200 characters; bio 150 characters
- **Image dimensions:** Feed post 1080 × 1080 px (square), 1080 × 1350 px (portrait, recommended), or 1080 × 566 px (landscape); Stories/Reels 1080 × 1920 px (9:16); carousel up to 10 slides
- **Best posting times:** Monday, Wednesday, and Friday 11 am–1 pm; Reels perform well in the evening (7–9 pm)
- **Hashtag conventions:** 3–5 highly relevant hashtags (Instagram's own recommendation); place in caption rather than first comment for best reach; mix of niche and mid-range tags
- **Content formats:** Feed posts (single image, carousel), Stories (24-hour ephemeral), Reels (up to 90 seconds, algorithm-boosted), Live, Guides
- **Audience expectations:** Visual quality is paramount. Carousels with educational or step-by-step content see the highest saves. Captions should be front-loaded — the first line must hook before the "more" truncation. Authentic, behind-the-scenes content outperforms polished stock imagery.
- **Engagement patterns:** Saves and shares are weighted more heavily than likes. Carousel posts generate higher engagement than single images. Reels reach non-followers far more effectively than feed posts. Respond to comments and DMs promptly — the algorithm rewards active accounts.

## Threads

- **Character limits:** 500 characters per post
- **Image dimensions:** Up to 10 images per post; recommended 1080 × 1080 px (square) or 1080 × 1350 px (portrait)
- **Best posting times:** Still emerging; early data suggests weekday mornings (8–10 am) and evenings (6–8 pm)
- **Hashtag conventions:** Minimal; the platform supports topic tags (single tag per post) rather than traditional hashtags
- **Content formats:** Text posts, images, links, carousel-style multi-image posts
- **Audience expectations:** Casual, conversational, community-oriented. The tone sits between X/Twitter and Instagram — less formal than LinkedIn, less visual than Instagram. Audiences reward genuine conversation starters and relatable observations. Overtly promotional content is poorly received.
- **Engagement patterns:** Replies and reposts drive visibility. Threads that spark back-and-forth discussion perform best. Cross-posting identical content from X/Twitter feels low-effort — tailor the tone.

## Facebook

- **Character limits:** Posts up to 63,206 characters; recommended length 40–80 characters for highest engagement
- **Image dimensions:** Feed image 1200 × 630 px (landscape) or 1080 × 1080 px (square); cover photo 820 × 312 px; Stories/Reels 1080 × 1920 px
- **Best posting times:** Tuesday to Friday, 9–11 am; Wednesday sees the most consistent engagement
- **Hashtag conventions:** 1–3 hashtags at most; Facebook's algorithm does not heavily reward hashtag use; they serve as topic markers rather than discovery tools
- **Content formats:** Text posts, images, video (native uploads outperform links), Stories, Reels, Live, Groups, Events
- **Audience expectations:** Community and conversation-focused. Facebook's audience skews older than Instagram or TikTok. Content that invites opinion-sharing, nostalgia, or practical advice performs well. Link posts to external sites receive reduced reach — native content is favoured.
- **Engagement patterns:** Shares are the highest-value interaction for reach. Comments (especially longer ones) signal quality to the algorithm. Video watch time matters — front-load the hook. Group posts often outperform Page posts for organic reach.

## TikTok

- **Character limits:** Captions up to 4,000 characters; most effective captions are under 150 characters with a hook
- **Image dimensions:** Video 1080 × 1920 px (9:16, full-screen vertical); photo carousels 1080 × 1920 px
- **Best posting times:** Tuesday, Thursday, and Friday 2–5 pm; Sunday 1–3 pm; consistency matters more than exact timing
- **Hashtag conventions:** 3–5 hashtags; mix trending and niche tags; avoid generic tags like #fyp or #foryou (they no longer influence discovery meaningfully)
- **Content formats:** Short-form video (up to 10 minutes, but 30–60 seconds performs best), photo carousels, LIVE, Stories, duets, stitches
- **Audience expectations:** Entertaining, authentic, fast-paced. The first 1–2 seconds must hook the viewer — retention rate is the primary ranking signal. Lo-fi, personality-driven content outperforms high-production material. Educational content ("Did you know…", how-tos, myth-busting) has strong evergreen potential.
- **Engagement patterns:** Completion rate (watching to the end) matters most for algorithmic distribution. Comments and shares amplify reach significantly. Trending audio can boost discovery, but only if used relevantly. Posting frequency (1–3 times daily) is rewarded more than on other platforms.

## Cross-Posting Rules

- **Never copy-paste identically across platforms.** Each platform has different norms for tone, length, and format. What reads as insightful on LinkedIn may feel stiff on Threads.
- **Adapt the hook.** LinkedIn hooks tend to be story-driven ("I made a mistake last year…"); X/Twitter hooks are punchy and declarative; TikTok hooks are visual/verbal within the first second.
- **Reformat, don't just trim.** A LinkedIn carousel can become an X/Twitter thread, an Instagram Reel script, and a TikTok talking-head video — but each version should feel native to its platform.
- **Stagger your posting times.** If you post the same core idea across platforms, space them out by at least a few hours. Audiences that follow you on multiple platforms will notice simultaneous identical posts.
- **Respect platform-specific media requirements.** A landscape image built for Facebook will be cropped awkwardly in Instagram Stories. Always resize and reframe.
- **Link handling varies.** LinkedIn and Facebook allow clickable links in posts; Instagram does not (use bio link or Stories link sticker); X/Twitter links reduce reach — consider putting the link in a reply instead.
- **Hashtag strategies do not transfer.** What works on Instagram (multiple niche tags) is counterproductive on X/Twitter (where 1–2 is the norm) and largely irrelevant on Facebook.
